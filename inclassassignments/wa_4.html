Reflection

My design system reflects parts of my identity through its color and typography choices. The purple palette I chose feels playful yet calm, a balance I associate with how I approach both creative work and life. Purple has also been my favorite color since I was little. The shades range from deep tones to soft pastels to contribute to contrast. For typography, I selected Sour Gummy because of its rounded letterforms. It feels authentic to me because it avoids looking too rigid or formal, which develops personality and makes the system feel approachable and human rather than overly professional. For accessibility, I had to check my color combinations to ensure text was always legible, even against the lighter purples. Some of my initial choices were too light, which meant I had to adjust shades to meet the WCAG contrast standards. This sometimes felt like a trade-off between pure aesthetics and usability. But ultimately, knowing that anyone can read and interact with the system without straining makes the design choices feel more complete. Accessibility had me make more conscious decisions instead of choosing based on pure interest. What surprised me most about building a design system was how many small decisions had to align to create cohesion. Spacing, buttons, and headings all feel minor on their own, but once I organized them under consistent rules, they suddenly made the page feel more unified. I can now carry this system directly into my portfolio, giving it a structured foundation while still leaving room for my personal design choices. In the future, I could expand this system by adding more detailed components like navigation bars, cards, or even animations to create more interactive experiences. I would also refine my documentation so others could understand and use the system without explanation. The most flexible parts seem to be the colors and spacing, since they can scale to different contexts easily. The least flexible element is the typography, since the playful nature of Sour Gummy may not always fit professional or minimal projects. Still, this limitation also helps me define when and where the system best represents me.
